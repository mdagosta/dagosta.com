Inbox Zero using Gmail

Email isn't broken

When I was a kid, I hand-wrote letters on paper to grandparents, friends and even pen-pals who I never met. It was fun to communicate and learn new things. So naturally when I got an email account in 1996, I couldn't be more excited. Email opened me up to communicating with so many more people, so much faster!

Now in 2013, with thousands of internet services competing for my mindless zombie attention, I find even more solace in Email - a one to one communication without the merciless injection of gossip and nonsense commonly found on the web these days.

These days a lot of people say that email is broken, and I disagree. I can compose an email to my Mom and Dad by clicking "Compose", typing "Mom" and tab-completing, typing "Hey" in the subject line, and write "How are you guys doing? All's well here" and attach a pic of my kids, in about 30 seconds. The next day, I'll get an overjoyed response about the wonderful pic, etc, etc.

I'm using Google Apps for my email, and I'm using just about all the features. With the right combination of filters, labels, stars, discipline and enjoyment of communicating, I see the glorious Inbox Zero screen 2 or 3 times a week. And when I do, I sit back, take a deep breath, enjoy the wonderful scenery and bask in the glory of what email can do.

Granted, I used to be overwhelmed by the sheer volume of email I was receiving. Until I started changing the way I feel about email. Primarily, I began to really love deleting it.

Seriously, deleting email has changed my life, and believe me, I delete a lot of it. Anything I won't need after 30 days, reply and delete. Gmail will thread it out of the trash if the other person responds. Ad nauseam.

For anything that needs to stick around, make sure it gets a label or three, and goes right into the archive. Needs a response? Star it with one of three stars which embody some meaning, and archive it. The Starred list is the todo list, which for me, as a husband, father, and programmer, hovers around 25 tasks or one screen full.

Anyone who is blood related automatically gets the "Family" label added, wife gets her own label. Anything that's send as bulk mail gets labeled bulk and archived, so I can go through my bulk mail in peace once a week. Old friends, hobbies, miscellaneous info that I'm desperate to save all go into a Personal label. Code Bug is a company that I run in the off-hours as a serious hobby, and I don't want it to disrupt dinner, so a lot of that gets labeled too.

So to achieve inbox zero using gmail, commit to a few months of setting up your system:

  1. Consolidate everything onto gmail
  2. Set up 8-15 labels with colors that will emcompass everything
  3. Set up a thousand filters to label everyone and everything automatically
  4. Sort and delete a hundred historic emails at a time
  5. Automatically sort bulk email and check it once a week
  6. Star anything that is a real action item / todo
  7. Use the inbox as a stream to read, then power through a couple dozen, send-and-delete style
  8. Let go of your emotions, write briefly if you're feeling it...
  9. And most importantly, delete with abadon!!
